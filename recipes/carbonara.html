Carbonara

Carbonara is a beautiful, classic Italian pasta that’s so creamy, you’d swear there’s a good amount of cream in it. And indeed, there’s plenty of recipes that cheat by adding in cream.

Carbonara Ingredients

  • Guanciale
  • Parmigiano
  • Egg yolks
  • Spaghetti
  • Pepper
  • Garlic

How to make Carbonara

  1. Cut the guanciale into thick batons
  2. Finely grate the parmiggiana reggiano
  3. Whisk together the egg, cheese and pepper in a large bowl
  4. Bring 4 litres (4 quarts) of water to the boil with 1 tablespoon of salt.
  5. Just before draining, scoop out one cup of pasta cooking water.
  6. Cook guanciale until golden while the pasta is cooking.
  7. Tumble the hot pasta into the pan with the guanciale then toss so the pasta gets coated in the guanciale fat.
  8. Tip the hot pasta into the bowl with the egg and use a rubber spatula to scrape out every drop of the guanciale fat into the bowl.
  9. Add 1/2 cup pasta cooking water into the bowl.
  10. Mix vigorously with the handle of a wooden spoon, spinning the pasta around, for around 30 seconds to 1 minute.
  11. Serve immediately in warm bowls. Pasta waits for no one!
